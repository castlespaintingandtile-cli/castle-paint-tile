## Multiple Signals Strategy

### The Most Important Signals:

- Creates immediate brand association

## What is a SERP?

**SERP = Search Engine Results Page**

### What It Shows:
- Organic search results
- Google Maps/local pack
- Paid ads
- Featured snippets
- "People also ask" sections
- Related searches

### Your Goal in the SERP:
- Rank #1 in organic results (main goal)
- Appear in Google Maps/local pack (secondary goal)
- Own the featured snippet if possible
- Get multiple page rankings (homepage + service pages)

### Example SERP for "Epoxy Flooring Phoenix"

```

────────────────────────────────────────────
Google Maps Pack (3 local businesses)
────────────────────────────────────────────

   Expert Epoxy Flooring Services...

 2. competitor-site.com ★★★★☆
   Phoenix Epoxy Flooring...

 3. another-competitor.com ★★★☆☆
   Commercial & Residential...
────────────────────────────────────────────
[People also ask] [Related searches]
```

## Why Multiple Signals Matter

**Single Signal = Weak**
- EMD alone → Google thinks "maybe relevant"

**Multiple Signals = Strong**
- EMD + relevant content + local signals → Google thinks "definitely relevant"

**Full Signal Portfolio = Dominant**
- All signals aligned → Google thinks "this IS the brand for this query"

### Signal Strength Pyramid

```
        BRAND DOMINANCE
           ↑
    USER BEHAVIOR SIGNALS
           ↑
      LOCAL SEO SIGNALS
           ↑
    CONTENT RELEVANCE
           ↑
  EXACT MATCH DOMAIN (Foundation)
```

Each signal reinforces the others, creating an unbreakable association between the search query and your brand.
